Allen the Alien

Allen and Byte are Earth’s newest visitors and Earthlings seem very friendly…or do they? Earthlings are sure to love a supersonic horn and x-ray gun…right? Allen and Byte just want to play, but some Earthlings aren’t quite ready for visitors like them!

Allen the Alien is a story about understanding and accepting differences. The simple text tells the story from Allen’s point of view, while the Illustrations reveal a completely different situation — the Earthlings are terrified of aliens! Readers will love the hilarious reactions of the Earthlings to Allen’s shenanigans, that are captured in the wonderfully quirky illustrations. With a unique artistic style and fantastic rhythm, Allen the Alien is a humorous and multi-layered read.

Arabella and the Magic Pencil

Arabella is a beloved only child who has a picture perfect life until her brother, Avery, arrives. While she loves him, it’s sometimes hard to like him. She spends her days creating marvelous things with her magic pencil, and ignoring him. But when Avery spoils a perfectly proper tea party, Arabella erases him from her life. Oops! How can she get him back?

About the Author

Stephanie Ward is a children’s book author and tireless traveler.

After many years in marketing, Stephanie now spends her time writing sweet, silly and side-splitting stories for children. American born, but a nomad at heart, she currently lives in London, England with her family.

Stephanie Ward is the author of picture books:

  • Allen the Alien, illustrated by Aleck Morton, published by Riveted Press
  • Arabella and the Magic Pencil, illustrated by Shaney Hyde, published by EK Books
  • Wally the Warm-Weather Penguin, illustrated by Vanessa Landin, published by Forwards Press
  • 5 Minute Christmas Tales, a collection of short stories in verse,illustrated by Karen Saaverdra, published by Bookoli
  • Hello Shark!, illustrated by Bethany Carr, published by Imagine That
  • Clownfish Aren’t Funny and Don’t Laugh Giraffe!, illustrated by Carrie Hennon and Brad Hunt, published by Imagine That

Stephanie is also a book reviewer for Armadillo Magazine and The Children’s Book Review.

Stephanie is represented by literary agent Erzsi Deàk at Hen & Ink Literary.
